# CrewFlow Sales Conversion Playbook

CrewFlow should be sold as an operations money engine for cleaning and home-service businesses, not as another SaaS dashboard.

## Core Offer

Position the product as:

```text
AI-powered operations assistant for service businesses.
```

The practical promise:

```text
Stop losing jobs, unpaid invoices, and follow-up opportunities in the daily rush.
```

## Best First Customer

Start with cleaning and home-service operators because they have:

- frequent repeat bookings
- WhatsApp-heavy communication
- field staff coordination
- invoices and collections gaps
- high missed-call and slow-reply pain

## Pricing Anchor

Use monthly subscription plus setup fee.

| Plan | Monthly | Setup | Best fit |
| --- | ---: | ---: | --- |
| Launch | $199/mo | $300 | Owner-led teams with 1-2 crews |
| Growth | $349/mo | $750 | Most serious teams with 3-8 crews |
| Scale | $499/mo | Custom | Larger teams with deeper automation needs |

Do not lead with discounts. Lead with operational recovery:

- one recovered job can cover the month
- one collected overdue invoice can cover setup
- weekly admin time saved compounds quickly

## Landing Page Flow

The public website should move in this order:

1. Pain: missed jobs, admin chaos, unpaid work.
2. Money engine: capture, book, dispatch, collect.
3. Product proof: receptionist, workflow, operations visibility.
4. Pricing: clear packages with setup.
5. Setup intake: create the configured workspace.

## CTA Rules

Primary CTA:

```text
Start setup
```

Secondary CTA:

```text
View pricing
```

Avoid vague CTAs like "Learn more" or "Get productive."

## First 10 Customer Sales Motion

Use white-glove onboarding.

1. Find local cleaning/home-service businesses.
2. Ask about missed calls, no-shows, unpaid invoices, and staff confusion.
3. Demo the receptionist and revenue-risk dashboard.
4. Offer setup as a done-with-you implementation.
5. Import services, staff, customers, and reminder templates manually.
6. Check in weekly until the business depends on the workflow.

## Qualification Questions

Ask before selling:

- How many inquiries do you miss each week?
- Where do new leads come from today?
- Do customers book through WhatsApp, phone, Instagram, or website?
- How do staff know where to go and what to do?
- How many invoices are unpaid right now?
- What follow-up currently gets forgotten?

## Close

The close is not "buy software." The close is:

```text
Let us configure the first workflow that captures new jobs, reminds customers, dispatches staff, and follows up on unpaid work.
```
